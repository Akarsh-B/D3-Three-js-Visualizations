How has COVID-19 changed our spending habits?

This visualization is taken from The New York Times, published on April 16, 2020

Why I like this Visualization?

The graph above shows the percentage change in consumer spending by category for the week of March 26 through April 1, 2020 over the same week in 2019. The graph is based on data on credit and debit cards purchases of nearly six million people in the United States, as tracked and analyzed by Earnest Research.

The graph shows very well how things changed during the 2020 quarantine, As seen from the graph during the "Stay at Home", there was huge boom in the sale of Gaming Accessories and Alcohol and people started ordering groceries online. Because of the Fast food centres being closed, lot of them moved towards online food delivery.

The Airlines, Movies and Cruises got hit because of the Social Distancing measures. The sales of products like Mobiles and general merchandise remained the same.

This visualization clealy explains how there the trend of spending changed due to quarantine and by how much percentage it changed over the year. The size of the data visualized is also a great measure to understand industry's sales duing the period. Overall the aesthetics looks good. The color codes used are visually appealing and its a great way to visualize the data of the topic of interest.
